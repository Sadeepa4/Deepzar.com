🔬 Neocortex and Cortical Columns: The Architecture Behind Human Intelligence

Published: July 8, 2025

Intro: Comfort Kill Your Dreams.

🔹 What is the Neocortex?

The neocortex is the most evolved and powerful part of the human brain — responsible for high-level thinking, abstract reasoning, language comprehension, imagination, and predictive capabilities...

🔹 What are Cortical Columns?

  • Each cortical column processes incoming sensory data
  • Builds a model of its part of the world
  • Predicts what’s likely to happen next
  • Compares real-time input with its prediction
  • Signals either a match (confirmation) or a mismatch (surprise)

⚙️ How Do Cortical Columns Work?

  • Receive real-time sensory input
  • Integrate with memory (temporal context)
  • Generate predictions about the immediate future
  • Compare input vs. prediction: match or mismatch

This system allows us to predict the world before we experience it, saving massive cognitive energy.

🧠 A Real-World Example: “There’s a Tree to My Left”

Let’s say you’re walking down a familiar path... your cortical columns responsible for spatial memory and vision predict its presence.

🧠 Why Prediction Matters: Stability vs. Surprise

  • Less energy spent processing input
  • Stable environment helps the brain focus on important surprises
